I made a decision...

Dear Diary,

There's a girl, me, Anisha. I live in a small town where everyone thinks they know everything about each other. But no one really knows what's going on behind my smile.

My life isn't what it seems. It all started when I was just a kid. I saw something terrible, something that took my parents away from me. Those memories haunt me like a shadow, darkening every corner of my mind.

After the accident, I went to live with my aunt and uncle. They tried their best, but nothing could fill the emptiness inside me. At school, I was an outcast. Bullied for being different, for carrying a weight that no one else could see.

Days turned into weeks, and weeks turned into months, but the sadness never left me. It was like a heavy fog, suffocating and relentless. I felt like I was drowning in a sea of sorrow, with no one to throw me a lifeline.

But amidst the darkness, there was one glimmer of hope. My best friend, Meena. She was always there for me, a ray of sunshine in my stormy world. She made me laugh when all I wanted to do was cry, and she held me close when the pain became too much to bear.

And then came that night. The night I lost her too. A drunk driver, a split-second decision, and she was gone. Just like that, my world shattered into a million pieces.

Alone in my room, I poured out all my pain onto a piece of paper, a letter to Sarah that would never reach her. Then I found myself on that bridge, overlooking the river.

The wind whipped at my hair, and the cold bit at my skin, but I hardly noticed. With tears streaming down my face, I closed my eyes and took a deep breath. In that moment, I made my choice.

As the world around me faded into darkness, I finally found the peace I had been searching for. But for those left behind, my absence was a wound that would never fully heal.

And in the end, as I let go of everything, I whispered her name into the wind, hoping that somehow, someway, she would hear me.

Forever in my heart,
Anisha,
